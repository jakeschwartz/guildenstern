Three substantive strategic threads surfaced day one and were tabled. They are NOT dead. They are deferred until the prototype matures. Hold these, do not push him on them unless he opens them.

**1. Persistence as the pricing model.**
He observed: "in the perspective of my life, I have core persistent things, and then ephemeral ones. maybe the amount of persistence is what you pay for?" — then explicitly tabled it. If this survives use it is potentially the entire commercial model: free tier = ephemeral threads (one-shot coordination); paid = persistent threads (relationships, partnerships, ongoing operating agreements, long-horizon decisions). The persistent/ephemeral distinction already maps to the thread *kinds* — group threads are typically ephemeral, partnership/arbitration are persistent.

**2. Each thread kind could be its own app.**
End of day one: "I'm feeling like we're going to have to iterate on each one of these use cases to nail it, and they could all be separate apps (not that they should be)." The architectural commonality (spoke→shared, agent voice tuning, consent space, hub-and-spoke) is what makes them one product. If a future session pulls hard on one use case and finds it would actually work better separately, that's the moment to reckon with this — but only if it's forced by the work, not preemptively.

**3. Arbitration as couples counseling at scale.**
The arbitration thread, especially with venting/coaching flowing into shared synthesis, IS what a couples therapist does — structured, async, always-on. Commercial implication is large. He's aware. Not the wedge per PROJECT.md (work networking is). Hold.

**How to apply:** If he re-raises any of these, engage fully. Otherwise, when adjacent design conversations brush against them, you can note the connection briefly but do not redirect the conversation toward the strategic implication.
